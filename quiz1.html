Data and Information Questions

What can occur if information is produced from incorrect or incomplete data?
The information will not be accurate, and may not represent the whole picture.
Why is it important to ensure the data is accurate?
If the data is not accurate, it will not be useful for producing information.
What are the properties of usable data?
It should be: Relevant, Accurate, Free from bias, Reliable, Timely, Adequately sampled. (FAR RAT)
What is a common cause of inaccurate data?
Biased data collecting methods.
How can the accuracy of a primary source be determined?
You can look at how reliable and trustworthy they are, and compare to other sources.
With an example, explain the importance of timeliness in ensuring the quality and usability of data.
A good example would be opinions of technology. Technology evolves quickly, so data that was collected perhaps a few years ago will no longer be as relevant.
What influences the introduction of bias into data?
There are many causes of bias, but commonly bias will be introduced when the person providing data has a vested interest.
What is the difference between quantitative and qualitative data? Provide two examples.
Qualitative data is data that is categorical (e.g. brands or worded rankings), and quantitative is numerical data (e.g. number of people in a room, or time spent driving).
What strategies could be used when gathering quantitative data?
Asking people to provide numbers (e.g. times) or measuring data.

Why is it important to obtain permission when collecting data?
Because the information produced will likely be shared, and without permission, this is violating others' privacy.
What is the purpose of consent forms?
They allow the person completing a survey to get an understanding of the purpose of the survey, and how the data will be handled.
